In today's data-driven world, data analytics and cyber security are two of the most critical fields of study. Data analytics plays a vital role in extracting valuable insights from large and complex datasets, enabling organizations to make informed decisions, optimize operations, and gain a competitive edge. On the other hand, cyber security is paramount in protecting sensitive data and critical infrastructure from cyberattacks, ensuring the integrity, confidentiality, and availability of information systems.

The International Conference on Data Analytics and Cyber Security (DACS) aims to bring together researchers, practitioners, and industry experts from around the globe to explore the latest advancements and emerging trends in these two interconnected fields. The conference will provide a platform for sharing cutting-edge research, discussing real-world challenges, and fostering collaborations that will shape the future of data analytics and cyber security.

The previous versions of this conference (DACS) were conducted as an International Symposium along with top-tier conferences:

  • IEEE International Symposium on Security, Privacy, and Trust in the Internet of Things (1st SPTIoT 2017 in Sydney, Australia; 2nd SPTIoT 2018 in New York, USA; and 3rd SPTIoT 2019 in Rotorua, New Zealand) in conjunction with IEEE TrustCom.
  • International Workshop on Artificial Intelligence for Intelligent Network Management (4th AINet 2021, New York, USA) in conjunction with IEEE ISPA.
  • Subsequently, the 5th AINet 2022 and the 6th AINet 2023, both held in Torino, Italy, in conjunction with IEEE COMPSAC.

Call for Papers

DACS (International Conference on Data Analytics and Cyber Security) invites researchers and practitioners to submit original and high-quality papers that address the following themes, but are not limited to:

Track 1: Data Analytics

  • Machine learning and artificial intelligence for data analysis
  • Big data analytics and distributed computing
  • Data mining and knowledge discovery
  • Data visualization and decision support systems
  • Applications of data analytics in various domains

Track 2: Cyber Security

  • Network security and intrusion detection
  • Cryptography and access control
  • Malware analysis and threat intelligence
  • Risk assessment and vulnerability management
  • Cyber security in cloud computing and IoT environments
  • Cyber-threat intelligence and incident response
  • Blockchain and distributed ledger technologies
  • Zero-trust security models
  • Quantum cryptography and post-quantum security

Track 3: Emerging Technologies

  • Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning
  • Large Language Model (LLM)
  • Generative AI (Artificial Intelligence)
  • Big Data Management and Analytics
  • Cloud Computing and Distributed Systems
  • Internet of Things (IoT) and Edge Computing
  • Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and User Experience
  • Quantum Computing

Best Paper Award

Three best paper awards will be presented at this conference: one for the best regular paper, one for the runner-up in the best regular paper category, and one for the best student paper.
